Platform: PC • PS5 • XBOX • Nintendo Switch 2
Zero Parades is a narrative driven role playing game from ZA/UM studios, where you play a burnt out spy reconnecting with their old network to attempt an impossible assignment.

As lead UX designer at ZA/UM, I was tasked with developing the menus and gameplay of Zero Parades, for PC and console.

I took various features of the game from concept design, through wireframing and UX development, to the final rendered and animated UI. I was primarily focused on orientating the games existing features towards fulfilling the narrative goals, organizing and executing the menu team's workload, and developing the onboarding experience for existing features.

UX Feature Team

Coming into the project, I was given a team of 6 developers of different disciplines to lead, as the UX feature team. In this role, I prioritized and costed menus and gameplay elements based on urgency and scale, ensuring the specifications were in scope, and developed in verifiable stages from the start to the end of the pipeline.

In working with the art team, I translated concept art into a consistent visual design language, ascribing design patterns to relevant visual elements, in order to create a formal design language for the game. We maintained a dialoge with the art team throughout production to ensure the aesthetic was maintained.
