Disco Elysium Mobile is a mobile adaptation of award winning PC game Disco Elysium, an open world role playing game. You play as a detective solving both a murder, and the mystery of your own identity.

As a designer at ZA/UM, I was tasked with re-developing Disco Elysium to adapt the game for mobile.

The Challenge
The project began with deducing the most suitable way to bring Disco Elysium to mobile, enabling those without PC's and consoles to experience the story. Disco Elysium has over 1XXXXXX words, fully voice acted, as well as a soundtrack, however, because of decisions made while developing, we were unable to easily create a direct mobile port. This restriction opened an opportunity to re-develop Disco Elysium in a format which would be more suitable for mobile play; after all, mobile and PC players need very different things out of how the game is presented and interacted with. Elements of Disco Elysium's features would need to be tailored; 1. Exploration - While PC players will happly spend hours exploring, navigating from A to B in Disco Elysium can take longer than a mobile player would want to spend playing for a single sitting. 2. Story - The most important element needed to be kept front and center, but Disco Elysium's story experience is like diving into a box of lego, looking for interesting pieces and thinking about what to make with them. A PC player can casually alt-tab to a walkthrough or wiki and back. However, mobile play doesn't afford this level of patience. The player would need to be more aware of their goal and be distracted by perihperal information if they are to stay engaged. 3. Systems - Some of the systems and menus in Disco Elysuim are farmillar to RPG players, but alien to less hard-core mobile users. Players need to understand the systems at the moment they are relevant, and only with the requred information. for narrative fans, rpg fans, IP fans
Defining the Concept
After research, I created personas for our target player profiles to see how the game could solve a need and fit into their lifestyle. for developing into rough prototypes. My goal was to deliver something more like an interactive graphic novel. In terms of design changes, it was important to me that the game stay true to it's artistic vision, and kept free of the kinds of dark patterns you'd expect to find on mobile games. We would offer the player a premium experience, with a free demo so they understood what were buying. I wanted to keep Disco Elysuim's painterly aesthetic; while having a consistent fidelity, and fit the style of presentation. With the amount of painting that would be required, I wanted something like concept art - more loose [wolf among us, batman, telltale games] (give more of these) - defining the concept - fit for mobile - no dark patterns - high art - keeping what makes the experience, while fitting the format

Redesigning the format
(the level map) Redesigned the open world story into a series of levels, each containing a story goal, to be completed before the next is revealed. considering our audience, i streamlined the mechanics, as to make it easier to dip into the game for short periods. Navagation was removed, as it is a time sink, and hard to navagate on a phone Other pilars were the low dissruption (game events shouldn't distract the player from their current goal), as figuring out where to go next should not be the puzzle. Broke down all the disco content into descrete levels, more suitable for mobile. short play time, easy to monetize without being intrusive X. splitting up a list of dialogue options to viewing an option at a time worked to the strengths of the writing, as each option was like a punchline to the situation.
